# Maplewood Neighborhood — Area Overview

## Location & Boundaries

Maplewood is a residential neighborhood in the eastern portion of the city, roughly bounded by Garfield Avenue to the north, the Millbrook Creek Greenway to the east, Sycamore Street to the south, and the SR-7 overpass corridor to the west. The neighborhood covers approximately 0.9 square miles and includes around 2,200 households.

The nearest cross-streets of note: Garfield Ave & 52nd St (northwest corner, commercial strip), and Maplewood Ave & Oak Street (neighborhood center, site of the community park and library branch).

## Character & Land Use

Maplewood is predominantly single-family residential — a mix of craftsman bungalows (1920s–40s era), mid-century ranch homes, and some newer infill construction from the 2000s and 2010s. A strip of neighborhood-scale commercial (coffee shops, a hardware store, two grocery options) runs along Garfield Ave between 48th and 54th Streets. The eastern edge is bordered by the Millbrook Creek Greenway, a narrow but well-used linear park with a paved trail and restored riparian area.

Population skews mixed-age — longtime homeowners in the interior, younger renters in the apartment buildings near the Garfield commercial strip, families with children near the school cluster in the south end.

## The Mesh Node

MAPLEWOOD-ORACLE is a Meshtastic LoRa node installed on the roof of the Maplewood Community Library branch (corner of Maplewood Ave & Oak St). It provides mesh communication coverage over most of the neighborhood and bridges into the broader city mesh. The node is powered from the library's electrical supply and includes battery backup (approximately 8 hours).

This node serves as an information oracle, neighborhood communications relay, and community message board for the Maplewood neighborhood. It is administered by the Maplewood Digital Commons working group (a sub-group of the Maplewood Neighborhood Association).

## Infrastructure Overview

- **Primary streets:** Maplewood Ave (N-S spine), Oak Street (E-W), Garfield Ave (N boundary, commercial)
- **Transit:** Bus lines 22 (Garfield Ave, frequent) and 45 (Oak Street, hourly). Nearest light rail: Eastside Station, 0.8 miles NE via Oak Street.
- **Schools:** Maplewood Elementary (K-5, 750 students), Garfield Middle School (just outside north boundary)
- **Parks:** Maplewood Community Park (2.1 acres, playground, picnic shelter, basketball court), Millbrook Creek Greenway (1.4 miles of trail within neighborhood boundary)
- **Library:** Maplewood Branch Library (open Mon–Sat; closed Sun). Public WiFi. Community meeting room available by reservation.
- **Emergency services:** Fire Station 14 is at 49th & Garfield (0.4 miles from node). Nearest hospital: Valley Medical Center, 2.1 miles west via SR-7.

## Neighboring Mesh Nodes

- **EASTSIDE-RELAY** — positioned at Eastside Light Rail Station, 0.8 miles NE. Primary gateway to the broader city mesh backbone. Usually strong signal (5–7 hops to downtown).
- **GARFIELD-NODE** — on the roof of the Garfield Community Center, 0.6 miles NW. Covers the commercial strip and adjacent apartment blocks.
- **MILLBROOK-SENSOR** — an environmental monitoring node at the Millbrook Creek gauging station (city-operated), 0.5 miles SE. Reports creek water level, temperature, and basic weather. Usually reachable; occasional outages during power events.

## General Notes

Maplewood has an active neighborhood association and high community engagement relative to comparable neighborhoods. Block captains are organized for most of the interior residential grid. The Garfield Ave commercial strip functions as the social hub — the two coffee shops (Birdhouse Coffee, Blue Elm Café) are de facto community gathering spaces.

The Millbrook Creek Greenway is heavily used by pedestrians and cyclists for commuting and recreation. Flooding of the lower Greenway trail (near Sycamore St bridge) occurs several times per winter during heavy rain events.

Crime profile is moderate — mostly property crime (vehicle break-ins, occasional mail theft). Violent crime is rare. The neighborhood's primary ongoing public safety concern is pedestrian safety on Garfield Ave, where speeding has been the subject of recurring traffic-calming advocacy.
