# Organizations & Community Groups — Maplewood Neighborhood

A guide to the active organizations, working groups, and recurring programs in and around Maplewood. Last reviewed: April 2026. For resources (food bank, library, etc.), see local-resources.md. For seasonal events, see seasonal-notes.md.

## Governance & Representation

### Maplewood Neighborhood Association (MNA)
The primary formal body for neighborhood self-governance. Membership is open to all Maplewood residents and businesses. The MNA:
- Advocates to City Hall on planning, transportation, safety, and parks issues
- Manages the neighborhood community garden
- Organizes the annual Spring Clean-Up Day and the Halloween coordination
- Hosts monthly meetings (second Tuesday, 7pm, Maplewood Branch Library community room)
- Website: maplewoodna.org | Email: info@maplewoodna.org

**Current Board (2025–26):**
- President: Carmen Delgado (5200 block Maplewood Ave)
- Vice President: Robert Osei (Oak St area)
- Treasurer: Lisa Park
- Secretary: Tom Nguyen
- At-large members: Priya Sharma, David Hernandez, Maria Kowalski

**Block Captains:** The MNA maintains a block captain network covering most of the residential grid. Block captains are the first point of contact for neighborhood-level concerns. Contact the MNA to find your block captain or to volunteer as one (several blocks currently uncaptained: 5300–5400 block east of Maplewood Ave, Sycamore St south of 52nd Ave).

## Public Safety & Emergency Preparedness

### Maplewood CERT Team (Community Emergency Response Team)
Trained neighborhood volunteers ready to assist neighbors during major disasters (earthquake, extended power outage, infrastructure failure) when professional responders are overwhelmed. Current team: 14 trained members distributed across the neighborhood.

CERT capabilities: basic triage, light search and rescue, fire suppression (small), utility shutoff, neighborhood damage assessment, emergency communications relay.

**Key contact:** CERT Team lead is David Hernandez (also MNA at-large member). Annual training exercises are held in spring. New volunteer training through the city's Emergency Management office twice per year — next cohort: May 2026. Register at cityem.gov/cert.

**Emergency caches:** Two neighborhood emergency supply caches exist:
- Cache A: Behind Fire Station 14 (49th & Garfield) — accessible to CERT members
- Cache B: Maplewood Branch Library utility room — accessible via library and CERT leads

### Neighborhood Watch
Not a formal organization — the MNA coordinates an informal network. Block captains serve as the watch network anchors. Crime and suspicious activity should be reported to the non-emergency police line ((555) 743-2200) first; then logged to the community activity log on this node or the MNA email.

The neighborhood does NOT have or endorse any formal citizen patrol, and residents are asked not to confront or follow individuals based on appearances. Report, don't engage.

## Environmental & Green Space

### Friends of Millbrook Creek (FMC)
A volunteer group focused on the health of the Millbrook Creek Greenway corridor. Works in partnership with the city Parks Department.

Activities:
- Monthly invasive plant removal (English ivy, Himalayan blackberry, Japanese knotweed) — typically 3rd Saturday, 9am–noon, meet at the Oak St bridge access
- Annual salmon monitoring (October–December): coho salmon return to the lower Millbrook in fall; FMC conducts spawner surveys. Coho have been documented in the stretch through Maplewood since 2017 — a significant restoration success.
- Native replanting: red alder, Oregon ash, red-osier dogwood, hardhack, and other native riparian species installed each spring with city nursery stock.
- Creek water quality monitoring: quarterly bacterial and turbidity testing, results reported to city and the Millbrook Watershed Council.

**Contact:** friendsofmillbrook@maplewoodna.org | Monthly email list signup on the MNA website.

Status: Coho returns in fall 2025–26 — 31 adults confirmed in the Maplewood reach, a record high for this monitoring location. Spawner surveys run October–December.

### Maplewood Community Garden
Located in the south portion of Maplewood Community Park. 42 plots (standard 10x20 ft) plus 4 ADA-accessible raised beds. Managed by the MNA.

Season: April 1 – October 31. Annual plot fee: $50 (sliding scale assistance available through MNA). Plot lottery held each March; waiting list typically 20–30 households.

Rules: Organic practices only (no synthetic pesticides or herbicides). Plots must be maintained actively; abandoned plots are re-assigned after 30 days notice. Water access on-site April–October.

Garden coordinator: Priya Sharma (MNA at-large). Contact: garden@maplewoodna.org.

## Mutual Aid & Community Support

### Maplewood Mutual Aid Network (MMAN)
An informal but organized network that emerged during the pandemic (2020) and has remained active. Facilitates:
- Grocery/errand runs for homebound or ill neighbors
- Childcare sharing and swap circles
- Tool-lending library (managed independently from the hardware store, hosted in two garages on the 5100 block)
- Emergency supply sharing (meals, generators, other) during power outages or other events

**Contact:** MMAN Signal group (ask at the library or MNA for an invite link). Also reachable via MNA email. 47 active network participants as of January 2026.

### Community Fridge (Oak St & 51st Ave)
No formal organization — volunteer-run, decentralized. Anyone can take, anyone can leave. Primary restockers are MMAN volunteers. Contents change daily.

### Eastside Community Pantry (regional org, serves Maplewood)
Location: Millbrook Church, 803 Sycamore St
Service hours: Tuesday & Thursday 10am–1pm, Saturday 9am–noon
See local-resources guide for details. No affiliation with MNA — independently operated.

## Education & Youth

### Maplewood Elementary PTA
Very active — one of the higher-engaged PTAs in the district. Runs the school's equity fund (provides supplies, field trips, and after-school enrichment for students whose families can't afford them). Also coordinates volunteer reading programs and the school's annual science fair.

**Monthly meetings:** First Wednesday of each month, 6:30pm, school library. Open to all parents/guardians.
**Contact:** maplewoodpta@gmail.com

### Maplewood Kids Coalition
After-school programming and summer camps for K–8 students with a focus on outdoor education and the arts. Not affiliated with the school directly — programs run in the Community Park shelter and the library community room. Sliding-scale tuition. Scholarship funding available.

**Contact:** (555) 743-8810 | maplewoodkids.org

## Digital & Technology

### Maplewood Digital Commons
The working group that administers the MAPLEWOOD-ORACLE mesh node and related community digital infrastructure. A sub-group of the MNA.

Current activities:
- Operating and maintaining the MAPLEWOOD-ORACLE Meshtastic node
- Maintaining this knowledge base (knowledge files updated manually — contributions welcome via MNA)
- Exploring mesh-based emergency communication protocols with the CERT team
- Advocating for ISP fiber expansion to unserved blocks (northeast section)

**Meetings:** Irregular; typically aligns with MNA meetings with a standing agenda item. Tech-focused members welcome.

**Contact:** digital@maplewoodna.org

## How to Get Involved

The fastest ways to plug into the Maplewood community network:
1. Attend an MNA meeting (second Tuesday, 7pm, library) — no barrier to entry, just show up
2. Introduce yourself to your block captain
3. Join the MMAN Signal network (ask at the library)
4. Show up to a Friends of Millbrook Creek work party (3rd Saturday)
5. Message MAPLEWOOD-ORACLE on the mesh — this node is monitored by Digital Commons volunteers and can connect you with the right group for whatever you need
