# Bestiary — Common Dungeon Creatures

## Threat Tiers
- **Green (Levels 1–2):** Manageable for a fresh party
- **Yellow (Levels 3–5):** Dangerous for unprepared groups
- **Red (Levels 6–8):** Requires tactics and preparation
- **Black (Level 9+):** Legendary threats

---

## Green Tier Creatures

### Goblin
**AC:** 15 (leather + shield) | **HP:** 7 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Scimitar +4, 1d6+2 slashing; Shortbow +4, 1d6+2 piercing (80/320 ft)
**Ability:** Nimble Escape — Disengage or Hide as a bonus action each turn.
**Behavior:** Hunt in packs of 4–12. Flee when outnumbered or when their leader falls. Often set ambushes in narrow corridors. Bribable with food or shiny objects.
**Loot:** 1d6 copper pieces, chance of stolen goods, goblin chief carries 2d6 silver.

### Skeleton
**AC:** 13 | **HP:** 13 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Shortsword +4, 1d6+2 piercing (two attacks); Shortbow +4, 1d6+2 piercing
**Immunity:** Poison damage, exhaustion, most fear effects
**Vulnerability:** Bludgeoning damage (double damage from maces, hammers)
**Behavior:** Mindless undead. Guard designated areas or patrol routes. Attack anything living that enters their area. Clerics can Turn Undead against them effectively (DC 13 WIS save or flee).
**Loot:** Whatever they carried in life; often armor and rusted weapons.

### Giant Rat
**AC:** 11 | **HP:** 7 | **Speed:** 30 ft (swim 30 ft)
**Attack:** Bite +4, 1d4+2 piercing. If hits, target must make DC 10 CON save or contract Dungeon Fever (disadvantage on ability checks for 24 hours).
**Behavior:** Swarm in groups of 6–20. Usually passive unless cornered or their nest is disturbed. Dangerous mainly in numbers. Found in food storage areas, sewers, and lower dungeon levels.
**Loot:** Nothing. But their nests sometimes contain lost items from past victims.

### Kobold
**AC:** 12 | **HP:** 5 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Dagger +4, 1d4+2 piercing; Sling +4, 1d4+2 bludgeoning
**Trait:** Pack Tactics — Advantage on attack rolls if an ally is adjacent to target.
**Trait:** Sunlight Sensitivity — Disadvantage on attacks and Perception in bright sunlight. (Irrelevant underground.)
**Behavior:** Cowardly but dangerous in numbers. Lay traps (pit traps, needle traps, rope snares). Will retreat to prepare more traps rather than stand and fight. Often serve dragons or more powerful creatures.
**Loot:** 1d4 copper each; their trap chambers may contain stolen valuables.

## Yellow Tier Creatures

### Orc Warrior
**AC:** 13 (hide armor) | **HP:** 15 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Greataxe +5, 1d12+3 slashing; Javelin +5, 1d6+3 piercing (30/120 ft)
**Ability:** Aggressive — Bonus action to move up to speed toward a hostile creature.
**Behavior:** Aggressive fighters who charge into melee immediately. War parties of 4–8 plus one orc war chief. Chief has 3× HP and extra attack. Will not surrender; fight to the death unless outmatched 3-to-1. Can be intimidated before combat starts (CHA DC 16).
**Loot:** 3d6 silver, chance of a non-magical weapon, chief carries a small magic item (20% chance).

### Ghoul
**AC:** 12 | **HP:** 22 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Claws +2, 2d6+2 slashing (DC 10 CON save or Paralyzed 1 min); Bite +2, 2d8+2 piercing (only vs. paralyzed or incapacitated targets)
**Immunity:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Poison
**Behavior:** Hunt in packs of 3–6. Stalk and separate the party. Target the weakest-looking member first. The Paralysis ability is extremely dangerous — a paralyzed character is helpless to bite attacks. Clerics Turn Undead against them (DC 14 WIS save).
**Loot:** Bone-strewn lairs contain accumulated treasure from victims: 2d20 gold, jewelry, mundane equipment.

### Bugbear
**AC:** 16 (hide armor + shield) | **HP:** 27 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Morningstar +4, 2d8+2 piercing; Javelin +4, 2d6+2 (30/120 ft)
**Trait:** Surprise Attack — First turn in combat, deal extra 2d6 damage if target is surprised.
**Trait:** Brute — Melee weapons deal an extra die of damage (included above).
**Behavior:** Prefer ambush tactics. Often lurk around corners or ceilings and drop on parties. Solitary or in pairs. Frequently lead goblin gangs. Cunning but brutal; can be bargained with if you offer something worth their time.
**Loot:** 2d10 silver, chance of a potion.

### Shadow
**AC:** 12 | **HP:** 16 | **Speed:** 40 ft (hover; can pass through objects)
**Attack:** Strength Drain +4, 2d6+2 necrotic. Target's STR max reduced by damage taken. Target dies if STR reduced to 0 and rises as a shadow.
**Immunity:** Necrotic, poison; exhaustion, frightened, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, prone, restrained
**Weakness:** Radiant damage; sunlight; torch light (disadvantage on attacks within 5 ft of bright light)
**Behavior:** Solitary or pairs. Haunt areas where people died violently. Avoid bright light. Try to drain the strongest fighter first, then pick off the weakened. Magic weapons recommended; mundane weapons have disadvantage against them.
**Loot:** None. But they guard places where something valuable was buried with the dead.

## Red Tier Creatures

### Mimic
**AC:** 12 | **HP:** 58 | **Speed:** 15 ft
**Attack:** Pseudopod +5, 1d8+3 bludgeoning + Adhesive (Grappled, DC 13 STR to escape); Bite +5, 1d8+3 piercing
**Trait:** Shapechanger — Takes the form of any object it has seen. The chest mimic is classic; door mimics and floor mimics are recorded.
**Trait:** Adhesive — Any creature touching the mimic's surface is Grappled (DC 13 STR to escape).
**Detection:** DC 14 Perception to notice something is off before touching it.
**Behavior:** Ambush predator that waits motionless. Once prey touches it, it attacks. Will attempt to swallow smaller creatures. Cannot be reasoned with — purely predatory. Found anywhere treasure might plausibly appear.
**Loot:** Accumulated bones and coins inside: 3d20 gold, possible magic item.

### Troll
**AC:** 15 (natural) | **HP:** 84 | **Speed:** 30 ft
**Attack:** Claws +7, 2d6+4 slashing (two attacks); Bite +7, 1d6+4 piercing
**Trait:** Regeneration — Regains 10 HP at the start of each turn. Regeneration stops if the troll was hit by fire or acid damage since its last turn.
**Immunity:** Only truly dies if reduced to 0 HP AND fire or acid damage was dealt.
**Behavior:** Territorial. Fights until reduced to 0 HP, then keeps regenerating unless fire/acid is applied. Fire is the key. Carrying oil flasks or fire spells is essential. Trolls guard bridge-like chokepoints and collect shiny objects.
**Loot:** Pile of 5d10 gold, 50% chance of a magic item dropped by a previous victim.

### Basilisk
**AC:** 15 (natural) | **HP:** 52 | **Speed:** 20 ft
**Attack:** Bite +5, 2d6+3 piercing; Petrifying Gaze (see below)
**Trait:** Petrifying Gaze — At the start of each creature's turn within 30 ft, if they can see the basilisk's eyes: DC 12 CON save. Fail = Restrained (slowing stone creep). Fail second consecutive save = fully Petrified (statue). Averted gaze = Disadvantage on attacks but immune to gaze.
**Cure:** Basilisk blood applied to a Petrified creature can reverse the effect. A Stone to Flesh spell works too.
**Behavior:** Territorial predator that hunts by proximity. Does not pursue far from its lair. Often found in stone corridors because its petrification creates more "scenery."
**Loot:** Petrified victims still hold their equipment and coins.

## Black Tier Creatures

### Beholder
**AC:** 18 (natural) | **HP:** 180 | **Speed:** 0 ft (hover 20 ft)
**Attack (Central Eye):** Antimagic Cone — 150-ft cone in front; all magic, including spells and magic items, is suppressed within the cone.
**Attack (Eye Rays, 3 per turn):** Rolls randomly from 10 options including: Disintegrate (10d8+40 force, DC 16 DEX or die), Sleep Ray (DC 14 WIS or unconscious), Petrification Ray (DC 16 CON or Petrified), Charm Ray (DC 16 WIS or Charmed), Fear Ray (DC 16 WIS or Frightened), Telekinesis Ray (DC 16 STR or flung 30 ft).
**Behavior:** Megalomaniacal and paranoid. Considers itself the pinnacle of creation. May parley if you offer useful information or if you genuinely flatter it. Has complex politics with other beholders and considers them abominations (all beholders disagree on what the "ideal" form looks like).
**Loot:** Its lair contains the equivalent of a medium noble's treasury: 5,000–15,000 gold, 3–6 magic items.

### Dragon (Young)
*Full stat block varies by dragon type. Representative: Young Red Dragon*
**AC:** 18 (natural) | **HP:** 178 | **Speed:** 40 ft (fly 80 ft)
**Attack:** Bite +10, 2d10+7 piercing; Claws +10, 2d6+7 slashing (two); Fire Breath (Recharge 5–6): 30-ft cone, 16d6 fire (DC 18 DEX save, half on success).
**Behavior:** Intelligent and proud. Will demand tribute before attacking if approached properly. Hoard-obsessed; threatens them economically rather than militarily for best results. Adult and Ancient dragons are effectively unkillable without a large, well-equipped party.
**Loot:** The hoard: 20,000–100,000 gold equivalent in coins, gems, and magic items.

## Special Encounter: The Dungeon Boss

Most major dungeons have a **Boss Encounter** at the deepest level. Boss traits:
- Legendary Resistance: Can choose to succeed on 3 failed saving throws per day
- Legendary Actions: Can take 3 extra actions at the end of other creatures' turns
- Lair Actions: Environment itself attacks on initiative count 20

Boss XP is worth completing an entire dungeon tier.
