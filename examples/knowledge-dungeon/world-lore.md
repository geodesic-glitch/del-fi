# The Shattered Realm — World Lore

## The Setting

The Shattered Realm is a world fractured by an ancient cataclysm known as the Sundering — when the god-king Malachar attempted to ascend to divinity and detonated his own soul across the continent. Magic became wild and unpredictable. Great cities collapsed into the earth. Dungeons erupted from the crust as the land folded over old vaults, temples, and ruins.

Centuries have passed. Civilization has rebuilt itself in walled city-states and trade towns. Adventurers — called "Delvers" — make their living recovering artifacts, clearing monsters, and mapping the labyrinthine underworld.

## The Five Regions

**The Ashfen Lowlands** — Swampy flatlands south of the Ironspine Mountains. Remnants of the old empire are half-sunken in black water. Lizardfolk, undead, and plague-cultists dominate. Treasure hunters come for imperial relics; few return. The dungeon complex known as the Drowning Vault lies here.

**The Ironspine Mountains** — A jagged spine of peaks running north to south. Dwarven holds occupy the upper tunnels; deeper passages are contested by stone giants, fire drakes, and the Forge Cult (a brotherhood seeking to relight the Sundering Forge). The Dungeon of Kharrak the Undying is located in the middle peaks.

**The Verdant Tangle** — Dense old-growth forest covering the eastern quarter of the realm. Elven communities live in the canopy. Below the root layer are ancient elven crypts and fungal cavern networks. The Mycelium Labyrinth is the most notorious dungeon here — the mushroom-minds within are said to absorb memories.

**The Pale Steppe** — Windswept grasslands to the north. Nomadic human tribes and hobgoblin war-bands clash constantly. Beneath the steppe, the ruins of the god-king Malachar's original throne complex — the Sunken Basilica — await anyone bold enough to descend.

**The Ember Coast** — A volcanic coastline to the west. Pirate towns, sea-trader guilds, and remnants of a fire-worshipping culture. The Ashen Crypts under the coastal cliffs hold pyromancer tombs and sleeping fire elementals.

## Major Factions

**The Delver's Guild** — The professional organization for adventurers. Grants licensed access to registered dungeons, provides insurance (resurrection bounties), and mediates contract disputes. Guild halls in every major city. Joining requires a sponsor and a completed dungeon run.

**The Cartographer's Society** — Obsessive mappers of the underworld. They pay well for accurate dungeon maps and often hire Delvers as escorts. Their maps are the most reliable in the realm — but they never share originals.

**The Restoration** — A religious order dedicated to repairing the damage from the Sundering. Oppose uncontrolled magic use and will pay bounties for magical artifacts brought to them rather than sold. They are not always friendly to Delvers who traffic in dangerous relics.

**The Grave Consortium** — A criminal organization that runs black-market artifact trading. They operate through front businesses in major cities. Selling to them pays more but carries legal risk and occasional professional hazard.

**The Sundering Scholars** — Academic researchers based at the University of Caldenmere. Hire Delvers for academic expeditions, pay fairly, and usually stay out of the way. Occasionally their curiosity gets everyone killed.

## The Underworld (The Deeps)

The dungeon network is not merely caves — it is the fractured remains of entire civilizations. Common underworld layers:

- **Shallow Deeps (Level 1–3):** Recently collapsed ruins, goblin warrens, small beast lairs. Manageable for new Delvers.
- **Mid Deeps (Level 4–7):** Ancient temples, elemental nodes, undead strongholds, active monster societies. Requires experienced parties.
- **Deep Deeps (Level 8–10):** Pre-Sundering vaults, demon-touched ruins, god-king remnants. Extremely dangerous; the laws of nature sometimes do not apply.
- **The Below:** Mythical depths below the Deep Deeps. No confirmed return from Level 11+. The Delver's Guild marks such expeditions as "presumed lost."

## Magic in the Realm

Magic is real but unstable. Most practitioners study one of three schools:

**Runecraft** — Inscribing stable magical formulae. Slower but reliable. Favored by dwarves and scholars.

**Soulweaving** — Drawing on personal life-force to power spells. Fast and flexible but physically taxing. Common among elves and witches.

**Void Touching** — Channeling energy from the Sundering's residual tear in reality. Powerful and unpredictable. Looked at with suspicion. Highly effective in the Deep Deeps where the Void bleeds through.

## Calendar & Time

The realm uses the Sundering Calendar — Year 0 being the Sundering itself. The current year is 412 AS (After Sundering). Seasons follow a standard cycle; the Ashfen Lowlands flood in spring, the Ironspine passes close in winter.

## The Node

DUNGEON-ORACLE is a magical construct — an enchanted stone tablet etched with responsive runes — located at the Delver's Guild outpost at Thornwall Crossroads, the primary staging town for expeditions into the Ironspine Mountains and the Pale Steppe. The Oracle draws from the Guild's accumulated knowledge logs, expedition reports, and bestiary records.
