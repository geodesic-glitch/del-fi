# Delver's Handbook — Game Rules

## Core Mechanics

### Checks & Dice
When the outcome of an action is uncertain, the Oracle calls for a **check**. Checks use a d20 (a twenty-sided die). Roll the die, add your relevant ability modifier, and compare to a **Difficulty Class (DC)**:

- DC 8 — Easy (routine for trained characters)
- DC 12 — Moderate (challenging for beginners, routine for veterans)
- DC 16 — Hard (requires real skill or luck)
- DC 20 — Very Hard (only experts succeed reliably)
- DC 24 — Near-Impossible (legendary feats)

**Natural 20** (unmodified roll of 20): Critical Success — something exceptional happens beyond just passing.
**Natural 1** (unmodified roll of 1): Critical Failure — something goes wrong, possibly beyond just failing.

### Ability Scores
Six core abilities define a character:

- **Strength (STR):** Melee attacks, carrying capacity, breaking things, grappling.
- **Dexterity (DEX):** Ranged attacks, dodging, stealth, lockpicking, acrobatics.
- **Constitution (CON):** Hit point maximum, endurance, poison/disease resistance.
- **Intelligence (INT):** Spell power (Runecraft), knowledge checks, trap analysis.
- **Wisdom (WIS):** Perception, survival, spell power (Soulweaving), willpower.
- **Charisma (CHA):** Persuasion, deception, intimidation, spell power (Void Touching).

Scores range from 3 (terrible) to 20 (exceptional human peak), with higher being better. Modifiers = (Score - 10) / 2, rounded down. Example: STR 14 = +2 modifier.

## Character Creation

### Starting Characters
New Delvers start at Level 1 with:
- One chosen class (see Classes)
- 8 + CON modifier hit points (minimum 1)
- Starting equipment package for their class
- One Dungeon Run credit with the Delver's Guild (apprentice status)
- Two skills from their class skill list

### Leveling Up
Characters gain experience (XP) from dungeon runs. XP milestones:
- Level 2: 300 XP
- Level 3: 900 XP
- Level 4: 2,700 XP
- Level 5: 6,500 XP
- Levels 6–10: each requires roughly double the previous tier

XP is awarded for: defeating monsters, bypassing traps cleverly, finding significant treasure, completing objectives, and exceptional roleplay.

## Combat

### Turn Order
At the start of combat, all participants roll **Initiative** (d20 + DEX modifier). Highest goes first; ties go to the higher DEX score.

### On Your Turn
A character can:
1. **Move** up to their Speed (typically 30 feet / 6 squares)
2. Take one **Action**
3. Take one **Bonus Action** (if available from class or ability)

**Common Actions:**
- **Attack:** Make one weapon attack (or two, if you have Extra Attack)
- **Cast a Spell:** Use a prepared spell
- **Dodge:** Impose disadvantage on all attacks against you until next turn
- **Help:** Give an ally Advantage on their next check
- **Hide:** Attempt a Stealth check to become hidden
- **Dash:** Double your movement this turn
- **Disengage:** Move without triggering opportunity attacks

### Attack Resolution
1. Roll d20 + attack modifier
2. If total meets or beats the target's **Armor Class (AC)**, the attack hits
3. Roll weapon damage dice + relevant modifier
4. Subtract from target's hit points

**Advantage / Disadvantage:** Roll two d20s, take higher (Advantage) or lower (Disadvantage). Sources include terrain, lighting, conditions, and abilities.

### Death & Dying
When hit points reach 0, a character falls **Unconscious** and begins making **Death Saving Throws** at the start of each turn:
- Roll d20: 10+ = one success, 9 or below = one failure
- Three successes: **Stabilize** (unconscious but stable)
- Three failures: **Dead**
- A natural 20: regain 1 HP and consciousness
- Taking damage while at 0 HP: one failure (or two if from a critical hit)

A Healer's Kit or stabilization spell stops the death countdown.

**Full Death** can be reversed by Resurrection magic (costly, see Items).

## Exploration

### Light & Darkness
The underworld is dark. Characters without darkvision (most races) are **Blinded** in darkness:
- Attacks against you have Advantage; your attacks have Disadvantage
- You fail any check that requires sight
- Torch: 20-foot bright light, 20-foot dim light, lasts 1 hour
- Lantern: 30-foot bright light, 30-foot dim light, lasts 6 hours (per flask of oil)
- Darkvision: See in dim light as if bright, darkness as if dim (no color). 60-foot range standard.

### Traps
Traps are detected with **Perception checks** (passive or active) and disarmed with **Sleight of Hand (DEX)** or **Arcana (INT)** checks depending on the trap type:
- Pressure plate trap: DC 14 to spot, DC 12 to disarm
- Tripwire: DC 10 to spot, DC 8 to disarm
- Magical ward: DC 18 Arcana to detect, DC 16 Arcana to safely suppress
- Poison needle lock: DC 14 Perception, DC 16 DEX to pick without triggering

### Resting
- **Short Rest (1 hour):** Spend Hit Dice to recover HP. Some class abilities recharge.
- **Long Rest (8 hours):** Fully restore HP and most resources. Can only benefit from one Long Rest per 24 hours. Resting in a dungeon risks a **Random Encounter** (roll d6 each hour; on a 1, roll the dungeon's encounter table).

### Doors & Locks
- Stuck door: DC 12 STR check to force
- Locked door: DC 14 DEX (thieves' tools) or DC 16 STR to break
- Reinforced door: DC 18 STR (may alert inhabitants)
- Arcane lock: DC 20 Arcana to suppress temporarily

### Mapping
Every 30 minutes of exploration in a new area, the GM (Oracle) may call for a **Dungeon Navigation** check (INT or WIS, DC varies by dungeon complexity). Failure means the party gets confused and may become lost or backtrack.

## Morale & Fear

Some monsters and magical effects force **Morale Checks** (WIS saving throw):
- DC 12: Mildly terrifying creature or situation
- DC 16: Truly horrifying sight (mass death, aberrations, seeing a god)
- DC 20: Reality-warping elder horror

Failure results vary by degree: Frightened (disadvantage on checks while source is visible), Panicked (flee for d4 rounds), Broken (incapacitated, drop equipment).

## Carrying Capacity

A character can carry up to 15 × STR score pounds comfortably. Above this, they are **Encumbered** (–10 ft speed). Above 30 × STR, they are **Heavily Encumbered** (–20 ft speed, disadvantage on DEX/STR/CON checks).

Standard item weights: Sword 3 lb, Shield 6 lb, Full Plate 65 lb, Torch 1 lb, 10 Rations 20 lb, Rope 50-ft 10 lb, Backpack contents vary.
